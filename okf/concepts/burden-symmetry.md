# Burden symmetry

The non-negotiable principle that makes Milcah fair, enforced mechanically (FR8):

- **Equal burden** — the *same* challenge structures are applied to every
  framework; **no framework is protected** for any reason.
- **Uncertainty is permitted; forced certainty is forbidden** — `unresolved`,
  `equivalent-burden`, `insufficient-information`, and `indistinguishable` are
  **valid terminal states**, made *more precise*, never collapsed.
- **Observations before frameworks** — start from observed phenomena where
  possible; frameworks explain observations, they don't own them.
- **Metrics exclude social signals** — popularity, confidence, and institutional
  acceptance never enter a coherence score.

These invariants govern [recursive pressure-testing](recursive-pressure-testing.md)
and the [coherence metrics](coherence-engine.md): the engine sharpens uncertainty
rather than resolving it artificially, and it judges every worldview by the same
rules. They are design invariants today; the mechanical enforcement (FR8) is part
of the as-yet-unbuilt round controller.
